Frequently Asked Questions

1. How far can you transport heavy-duty machinery and equipment?

We transport heavy-duty machinery locally within Chaparral, NM, to neighboring states, and nationwide. We plan the route, secure permits if required, and assign the right heavy-duty equipment to move your load safely and legally.

2. How do I know which towing or recovery service I need when I call Towing Service Chaparral, NM?

You don’t need to figure that out; it’s our job. When you contact us, a trained dispatcher asks the right questions about your vehicle, load, and situation. Based on that information, we determine whether you need light-duty towing, heavy-duty towing, winching, recovery, or specialized equipment. We send the correct truck and crew the first time.

3. Are your drivers and operators certified and professionally trained?

Yes. Every heavy-duty driver is CDL-certified, and our operators are professionally wrecker-trained with hands-on experience in towing, recovery, load securement, and roadside safety. We don’t send “just a driver” to complex jobs. Our crews are trained to handle accident scenes, heavy loads, off-road recoveries, and emergency situations while following state and federal safety regulations.

4. Can you move a big rig truck with the trailer still attached?

Yes, we can, as long as the situation allows it safely and legally. In many cases, we tow the tractor and trailer together to save time and reduce downtime. If the trailer is overloaded, damaged, jackknifed, or unsafe to move as one unit, we may separate it and tow each section properly. Our operators assess the scene first and choose the safest option that complies with Chaparral, NM and federal regulations.

5. Do you tow disabled vehicles?

Absolutely. Towing disabled vehicles is one of our core services. We have the right equipment to move and recover vehicles that won’t just move due to several reasons, which could be mechanical failure, electrical issues, or transmission problems, or because they were involved in an accident.

Chaparral, NM Insights: Population,
Zip Codes, Influence, and Service Areas

Chaparral is an unincorporated community and census-designated place (CDP) in Doña Ana and Otero counties, New Mexico. The population was 14,631 at the 2010 census. Chaparral is primarily a bedroom community for the neighboring city of El Paso, Texas, and the neighboring military installations of White Sands Missile Range and Fort Bliss. It is officially part of the Las Cruces Metropolitan Statistical Area.
